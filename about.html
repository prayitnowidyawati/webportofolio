People, Places, Perspectives - The way I learn, The way I grow, The way I teach

I teach languages, but honestly, it’s never just about language. And somehow, we (you and me) always end up learning about life too.

Because once you start learning a language, you don’t just learn words.

You start opening something deeper.
It’s not just vocabulary.
It’s expressions.
It’s confidence.
It’s those small moments when you understand…
and you smile because you get it.

And slowly, it becomes something more.
It feels lighter. More natural.
Almost like therapy,
but with laughter, stories, and a lot of “ ohhh now I understand”

You start opening doors.
To people.
To places.

It slowed me down. It made me reflect and choose more carefully how I want to live and what I want to give.
And from there, I didn’t just continue, I became more intentional.

That’s also why I created this space.
Not just as a website,
but as a reflection of my journey,
my work, and the way I see learning.

Because for me, learning is not separate from life.
It happens in conversations.
In traveling.
In tasting new food.
In listening to music from different cultures.

I love those moments.
And I bring that energy into how I teach.

how to connect,

you’ll enjoy it more than you expected

How I Got Here

Education has been a beautiful path in my life, one that began at a young age and has been shaped by wisdoms through the years. Surviving a major accident transformed my perspective and strengthened my determination to build a meaningful career in education, leading me to gain professional and international experience. For me, the classroom is not only a place to build professional portfolios but also an inspiring space where continuous self-development happens. I believe the true outcome of education is positive change—growth in mindset, behavior, and character. This belief forms the foundation of my work: supporting learning with respect for every individual’s unique journey and encouraging each person to move toward a better version of themselves.

My Values & Beliefs

Transformation- Resilience - Lifelong learning

I believe education is more than gaining knowledge; it is about becoming a better person. True learning helps shape character, build confidence, and create positive changes in behavior. Every individual has a unique journey, and meaningful education respects that journey while supporting continuous growth. Resilience and self-development are values that guide my life and work.

My experiences have taught me the importance of perseverance, empathy, and lifelong learning. Through education, I aim to create supportive learning spaces where people feel encouraged to grow, discover their potential, and move forward with confidence.

For me, the purpose of education is simple: to help people learn, grow, and become better versions of themselves while respecting every step of their journey.

Why Work With Me

Working with me means learning in an environment that is supportive, practical, and focused on real growth. I combine professional experience, international exposure, and a deep commitment to education to help individuals build confidence, clarity, and meaningful skills that last beyond the classroom.

I believe every learner has unique strengths and a different pace of development. My approach is personal, encouraging, and goal-oriented—designed to help you understand the material while also developing the mindset, discipline, and self-belief needed for long-term success.

Together, we focus on steady progress, creating a learning journey that is respectful, motivating, and truly transformative.

A few more words from me: Vive Plene (Live Fully)!

What makes life feel alive for you? For me, it’s travelling, exploring arts and culture, and spending time in the kitchen creating something delicious to share!.

Travelling opens my eyes to new perspectives, while art and culture keep my creativity flowing and my curiosity growing. Cooking is one of my favorite ways to relax and connect with people.

These simple passions remind me that learning happens everywhere—not only in classrooms, but also through the places we visit, the cultures we experience, and the joyful moments we create along the way.
